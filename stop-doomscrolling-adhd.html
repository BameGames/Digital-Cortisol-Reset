How to Stop Doomscrolling with ADHD: A Guide to Resetting Your Brain

Published: Jan 2026 • 6 min read • Topic: Digital Cortisol

We’ve all been there: you open your phone to check the time, and 45 minutes later, you’re deep in a rabbit hole of stressful news or short-form videos. For the ADHD brain, this isn’t just a bad habit—it’s a dopamine trap.

Why ADHD Brains Get Stuck

Doomscrolling provides a constant stream of "micro-rewards." Every new post gives your brain a tiny hit of dopamine. However, it also spikes your Digital Cortisol, leaving you feeling "wired but tired."

The "5-Minute Reset" Trick

The hardest part of stopping a doomscroll is the transition. Your brain needs a bridge between the chaos of the feed and the quiet of real life.

3 Fast Ways to Break the Loop

1. Use the "Greyscale" Hack

Social media apps are designed with bright colors to trigger your brain's reward system. By turning your phone to greyscale (Settings > Accessibility), you make the "doomscroll" significantly less rewarding.

2. Set a "Visual" Timer

ADHD often comes with time blindness. A digital clock is easy to ignore, but a physical visual timer shows you exactly how much "scrolling time" is left, helping you snap out of hyperfocus.

3. Replace, Don't Just Quit

Instead of trying to use willpower to "stop," give your hands something else to do. Use a fidget toy or a non-stimulating game that uses rhythmic patterns to lower your heart rate rather than raising it.
